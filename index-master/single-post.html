EDITORS CHOICE
POEM OF THE WEEK

Maya Angelou

TALK ABOUT WOMAN CRUSH

Maya Angelou, born April 4, 1928 as Marguerite Johnson in St. Louis, was raised in segregated rural Arkansas. She was a poet, historian, author, actress, playwright, civil-rights activist, producer and director.

She lectured throughout the US and abroad and was Reynolds professor of American Studies at Wake Forest University in North Carolina since 1981.

She published ten best selling books and numerous magazine articles earning her Pulitzer Prize and National Book Award nominations. At the request of President Clinton, she wrote and delivered a poem at his 1993 presidential inauguration.

Touched By An Angel

We, unaccustomed to courage

exiles from delight

live coiled in shells of loneliness

until love leaves its high holy temple

and comes into our sight

Love arrives

and in its train come ecstasies

old memories of pleasure

ancient histories of pain.

Yet if we are bold,

love strikes away the chains of fear

from our souls.

We are weaned from our timidity

In the flush of love's light

we dare be brave

And suddenly we see

that love costs all we are

and will ever be.

Yet it is only love

which sets us free.

to liberate us into life.

    Let’s pretend you’re a complete stranger.

    Let’s pretend our hearts don’t long to be with each other even though we’re so far apart.

    Let’s pretend my heart doesn’t race at the thought of your name.

    We can pretend but you and I both know it only makes the pain worse.

    Pain we try so hard to ignore but we can’t because it seems to be the only thing keeping us going.

    Could we pretend all our feelings were make believe?

    That they were just tales meant to be left in the pages of a child’s story book.

    That I could not be your princess who simply fell in love with one look.

    Oh no, clearly our story is far greater than that of Captain Hook.

    Can we pretend you’re made for someone else?

    That you have eyes only for her and my heart doesn’t break every time you say her name.

    Can we pretend we live in a world where I’m happy and you’re not even a thought that crosses my mind?

    If all this were true, you wouldn’t be back again after telling me you found the love of your life.

    I t’s never easy to walk away, but how do I walk away when we’re both not ready to let go?

    How do I let go when you constantly text me just when I think it’s over?

    Let’s play pretend.

    Let’s pretend we’re just friends because strangers is too far apart.

    Let’s pretend you’re happy to see me with him.

  1. Thabo Ravid Bwanali
    .....

    Time is sleeping on my watch,

    Time is slipping on my watch.

    Slow and steady, I wish its hands could be quickened,

    To the next hour or minute,

    Where in, my pain can not be experienced.

    Fast and shaky, I wish this present moment ticked more than a day long,

    So I can gyrate to my positive vibrations,

    Write a song, maybe a poem,

    Take a promenade with them who freeze time, imprinting vivid memories onto my mind’s canvas,

    But the hands of time are constrained, the least it could provide

    Is a hand full of experience.

    Time is a pandora box,

    Every one of it’s hands is curious,

    Curious as the sun, to shine on a rainy day

    Curious as a child eager to tread the earth,

    Curious as the moon waxing in full strength

    Curious as Life taking shape

    Curious as death!

    Curiosity furthers a thought,

    What if we lived outside of time?

    Well then we would be God,

    Perhaps we can, yes on a blessed day,

    When Time shall be no more.
